What is Digital ID?

Digital ID is a private beta iOS application that allows customers to carry a digital version of their Victorian driver's licence on their iPhone. Once set up, they can display their licence digitally instead of carrying a physical card.

Generating Payment Codes

When a customer pays you $70 AUD in cash, you need to generate a payment code for them. This is your main task as a distributor.

Step-by-Step: Creating a Code

1

Log into the Admin Panel

Go to your admin panel URL and sign in with your distributor credentials.

2

Go to "Payment Codes"

Click the Payment Codes tab in the navigation menu.

3

Add Notes (Recommended)

Type the customer's name or any identifier in the notes field. This helps you track who redeemed which code.

4

Click "Generate Code"

A unique 12-character code will be created instantly.

5

Give Code to Customer

Write it down, text it, or let them photograph it. Click "Copy" for easy copying.

What a Code Looks Like

ABCD-EFGH-JKLM

12 characters, separated by dashes, all uppercase

‚ö†Ô∏è Code Rules

  • One-time use only ‚Äî Each code works for exactly one customer
  • Expires in 30 days ‚Äî Unused codes become invalid after 30 days
  • Can be revoked ‚Äî You can cancel unused codes if needed
  • Tracked ‚Äî The system shows which email redeemed each code

Admin Panel Reference

The admin panel shows you all your generated codes and their status.

Code Status Types

Status Meaning Action
ACTIVE Code is ready to use, not yet redeemed Can be given to customer or revoked
USED Code has been redeemed by a customer No action needed ‚Äî shows who used it
EXPIRED 30 days passed without being used Generate a new code if needed
REVOKED You cancelled the code Cannot be undone

Admin Panel Shows You:

  • Active count ‚Äî How many unused codes you have
  • Used count ‚Äî How many codes have been redeemed
  • Code list ‚Äî All codes with status, notes, date, and who redeemed
  • Customer email ‚Äî Shows which email used each code

üí° Best Practice: Always Add Notes

When generating a code, add the customer's name in the notes field. Example: "John Smith - paid cash 13/02". This helps you track payments and resolve any issues later.

When to Revoke a Code

  • Customer changes their mind and wants a refund
  • You generated a code by mistake
  • Customer lost the code and needs a new one

Photo Guidelines

The photo appears on their digital licence. A good photo makes the ID look professional and legitimate. Help customers take a proper ID-style photo.

‚úì DO

  • Face camera straight on
  • Neutral expression (or slight smile)
  • Good, even lighting on face
  • Plain, light background
  • Face clearly visible
  • Remove sunglasses & hats
  • Hair away from face
  • Head and shoulders visible

‚úó DON'T

  • Selfies at weird angles
  • Filters or editing
  • Shadows on face
  • Busy or textured backgrounds
  • Blurry or dark photos
  • Other people in frame
  • Sunglasses or tinted lenses
  • Fashion headwear (beanies, caps)

Photo Examples Reference

‚úì Approved ‚úó Rejected
Neutral expression, mouth closed, plain background, good lighting Big smile showing teeth, mouth open
Plain, single-color background Textured wallpaper or patterned background
Clean background, nothing behind head Objects visible behind head (plants, furniture)
Religious headwear (hijab) ‚Äî allowed Fashion headwear (beanies, caps) ‚Äî not allowed
Hair accessories removed Sunglasses on head or hair clips visible
Even lighting, no shadows Shadow across face or shadow behind head

üí° Ideal Photo Setup

Have them stand about 30cm from a plain white or light-colored wall. Natural light from a window works best ‚Äî make sure the light is on their face, not behind them. Hold the phone at eye level, about arm's length away. Frame from shoulders up.

Common Photo Problems & How to Fix Them

If a customer's photo doesn't look right, here's how to help them fix it:

Problem Solution
Face too dark or shadowy Move them closer to a window or turn on more lights. Make sure the light source is in front of them (lighting their face), not behind them.
Blurry photo Hold the phone steady. Tap the screen to focus on their face. Make sure they stay completely still while the photo is taken.
Bad angle Hold the phone straight, at their eye level. Don't tilt up, down, or to the side. Think "passport photo" ‚Äî straight on.
Messy background Find a plain wall ‚Äî white, cream, or light grey. If no plain wall available, stand them further from the background so it blurs out.
Face too small in frame Move closer to them, or zoom in slightly (but not too much or it gets grainy). Head and shoulders should fill most of the frame.
Weird expression Tell them to relax, take a breath, and look directly at the camera lens (not the screen). A neutral face or tiny smile is fine.
Shadow behind head Move them further from the wall (about 50cm away). This separates them from their shadow.
Red eye Turn off the flash. Use natural light instead, or turn on room lights.

üí° Pro Tip: Take Multiple Photos

Encourage them to take 3-4 photos and pick the best one. It's much easier to choose from options than to try to get one perfect shot. They can retake as many times as needed before submitting.

If You're Taking the Photo For Them

  1. Find a plain, light-colored wall
  2. Position them about 30cm from the wall
  3. Make sure main light source is in front of them
  4. Hold phone at their eye level
  5. Frame from shoulders up, centered
  6. Tell them to relax and look at the camera
  7. Take 2-3 shots
  8. Show them and let them pick the best one

Signature Guidelines

The signature appears at the bottom of their digital licence. It should look like their real signature ‚Äî the one they use on official documents.

‚úì DO

  • Use index finger (works best)
  • Sign like they normally would
  • Use the full width of the box
  • Make it big and bold
  • Take their time ‚Äî no rush
  • Clear and redo if not happy
  • Rest hand on phone for stability

‚úó DON'T

  • Write name in plain letters
  • Make it tiny in one corner
  • Rush through it
  • Use pinky (too small)
  • Use thumb (too big/clumsy)
  • Accept a messy signature
  • Press too hard or too light

Signature Technique

1

Position the Phone

Hold the phone flat and stable on a surface, or rest it in their non-dominant hand.

2

Rest Their Hand

Have them rest the side of their signing hand on the phone. This provides stability and reduces shakiness.

3

Use Index Finger Pad

The soft pad of the index finger gives the best control and line thickness.

4

Sign Smoothly

Apply light, even pressure. Sign in smooth, flowing movements. Fill most of the signature box.

5

Review and Redo

Look at it. Happy? Submit. Not happy? Tap "Clear" and try again. No limit on retries.

üí° Pro Tip: Practice First

If they're struggling, have them sign their name on a piece of paper first. Look at that signature, then copy it on the phone. This helps them visualize what they're trying to create.

Common Signature Problems

Problem Fix
Too small Use more of the box ‚Äî make bigger, bolder strokes
Shaky lines Slow down, rest hand on phone, relax
Lines too thin Press slightly firmer with finger
Doesn't look like real signature Practice on paper first, then copy it

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions customers ask and how to answer them:

Q: How much does it cost?
A: $70 AUD, one-time payment. No subscriptions or recurring fees.
Q: Does it work on Android?
A: No, iPhone only at this time. Android is not supported.
Q: Does it work on iPad?
A: No, iPhone only. iPads are not supported.
Q: Can I pay with card online?
A: No, payment is through authorized distributors only. You pay the distributor $70 cash and receive a payment code.
Q: What if I get a new phone?
A: Contact support. We can reset your device binding so you can log in on your new phone.
Q: Can I change my photo or details later?
A: No. Once you submit your identity, everything is permanently locked. Make sure you're happy with everything before submitting.
Q: I made a mistake in my details. Can I fix it?
A: Contact support. We can reset your identity so you can start over, but you'll have to re-enter everything.
Q: Can my friend/family use my account on their phone?
A: No. Each account is permanently locked to one phone only. They need their own account and payment code.
Q: I forgot my password.
A: Use the "Forgot Password" link on the website login page to reset it.
Q: How long until I'm approved?
A: Usually within 24-48 hours. We manually review each application.
Q: Why do I need to be approved?
A: We manually review every customer to ensure security and prevent misuse of the system.
Q: My payment code isn't working.
A: Make sure you're entering all 12 characters including the dashes. Codes are case-insensitive. If it still doesn't work, contact the distributor who gave you the code.
